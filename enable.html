Who Are We?

The Enable team is one of the Biomechatronics Club mechanical design teams and we are taking on the task of designing and building a passive prostatic hand. The team mainly consists of students passionate about learning, the Biomechanical field, and changing the world.
The UW Enable team is based on “Enable Community Foundation”. The Enable Community Foundation aims to implement and validate a scalable high-tech infrastructure that matches recipients with volunteers who create open-sourced assistive technologies…for free or minimal costs. It supports educational materials and program to recruit and develop more digital humanitarians. And it is developing partnerships and programs to bring these inspirational new methods and solutions to the entire world.

Our Project

The Enable team’s main project is the designing and building of a passive prostatic limb. The current model the team is working on is an adaptation of “The Flexy hand”. The team’s current goal is the development and manufacturing of artificial joints and muscle to complete its project.

Join Us!

We currently meet weekly during both A-Society and B-Society terms and would love to have any new members come by and check out what we're up to. There's always more than enough work to be done and fun to be had. Anyone interested in participating in the Enable project should feel free to come by any of our meetings to hear about where we’re at in the project and what can be done to help out.
Almost any work that is available to be done on Enable, from mechanical design to production, research and or administration, is highly encouraged for new members to experiment with. Documentation and SolidWorks files can be made available to any individual in the group, and members can feel free to talk to executives and each other about new ideas regarding design and implementation.
