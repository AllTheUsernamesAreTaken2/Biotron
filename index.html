About Us

Our goal is to enable students to learn about and work with engineering systems that interface with the human body.
We accomplish this by creating a community of passionate students, centered around the field of biomechatronics. We host workshops and other learning events to provide students with the skills they need to do meaningful work, and we also provide students with the resources they need to start up their own projects in the field.

Currently, the biomechatronics club fosters 6 projects:

Beowulf
BCI
Powered Arm

Atlas
Enable
The Pillow Project

Details about them can be found on their respective pages.

Get Involved

Students

Get involved by joining our Facebook page! There we will post about all of our events, which are always open to anyone who's interested. Workshops are a great way to get involved, meet other club members, and to learn about topics from processing EMG data to coding neural networks.
