Who Are We?

The Beowulf team is one of the Biomechatronics Club's larger design teams (lol jokes) and we are taking on the task of building an electro-mechanical system which can assist people who have trouble with their grip. The team mainly consists of hardware designers, concept designers, and software developers, but the whole team is always consulted on any design or implementation decision made for Beowulf.

Our Project

Project Beowulf is the BioMechatronics Club’s first attempt at a fully functioning proof of concept design project in the field of biomechanics. The product is to be an exoskeletal tendoned glove to help individuals who are in need of assisted grip. It is intended for use by the elderly and disabled, however stretch goals such as military application are being kept in mind throughout development.
Beowulf’s current design is comprised of a 3D-printed exoskeleton, nylon tendons, force sensitive resistors (FSRs) and EMG sensors, a motor, and a passive tightening mechanism.

Join Us!

We currently meet weekly during both A-Society and B-Society terms and would love to have any new members come by and check out what we're up to. There's always more than enough work to be done and fun to be had. Anyone interested in participating in the Beowulf project should feel free to come by any of our meetings to hear about where we’re at in the project and what can be done to help out.
Almost any work that is available to be done on Beowulf, from mechanical design to production or software development to administration, is highly encouraged for new members to experiment with.
Documentation and SolidWorks files can be made available to any individual in the group, and members can feel free to talk to executives and each other about new ideas regarding design and implementation.
