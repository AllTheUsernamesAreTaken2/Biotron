Who Are We?

The Brain-Computer Interface Team is a group of people all interested in interacting with the human brain directly. Primarily, we develop algorithms to understand what's going on inside the brain my measuring the electrical impulses on the surface of the head. We use this data to allow users to control systems directly with their brains. This technology could be revolutionary in aiding patients with little to no motor control to live better lives.

Our Project

The BCI Team is currently working on a Brain-Computer Interface to compete in the Cybathalon competition in 2017. Our goal is to create a device patients with little to no motor control can use to interact with the world. The objective of the competition is have a disabled user control a simple obstacle course video game using our BCI. The team who achieves the best score on the mind-controlled game will win the competition.
